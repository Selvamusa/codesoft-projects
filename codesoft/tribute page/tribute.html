Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

About Him

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. He was also a beloved teacher and visionary, inspiring millions through his writings and speeches.

Achievements

  • President of India (2002–2007)
  • Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), Bharat Ratna (1997)
  • Key role in India's space and missile development programs
  • Author of books like *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and *India 2020*

Legacy

Dr. Kalam continues to inspire generations through his life, dedication, and words. His humility, vision, and belief in youth made him a true people's president.
